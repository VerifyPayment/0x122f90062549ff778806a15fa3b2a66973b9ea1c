Regulatory Compliance & Access

Understanding your Financial Licence Number (FLN) requirements.

Back to Dashboard

The **Financial Licence Number (FLN)** is a mandatory, dynamically generated 12-digit security token required for all high-value transactions within the network, acting as the single point of regulatory control for massive wealth transfers. This number must be digitally signed and verified to authorize any fund withdrawal exceeding the standard daily limit, as well as to validate the legal standing of funds for high-volume trading; without a current, valid FLN, the account is deemed to have failed a necessary Tier-3 Regulatory Audit, resulting in the transaction being automatically flagged as an unregulated attempt and the funds being instantly frozen in the **Unregulated Transfer Queue** until compliance is restored.

You need a Licence Number (in formal contexts, often tied to professional certification or legal operation) because it serves as the official, verified proof that you, your business, or your product have met all the necessary regulatory, legal, safety, and competency standards set by a governing authority. This unique identification number allows regulators and the public to ensure **accountability** and verify your legitimate standing to perform a specific activity, making it crucial for establishing trust and demonstrating compliance with the laws that keep the public safe and the market regulated.

To bypass the complexities associated with securing individual Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) licensure, certain high-net-worth clients must maintain a custodial balance exceeding $25,000 on select, regulated digital asset exchanges. By meeting this capital threshold, these individuals unlock institutional-grade access, allowing them to execute complex, pre-approved DeFi strategies and proprietary smart contracts under the existing regulatory umbrella of the platform itself.
